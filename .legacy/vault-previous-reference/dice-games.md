# Dice Games & Probability Practice

**Category:** Skills — Recreation & Mental Fitness
**Difficulty:** Beginner
**Time:** 5-30 minutes

---

## Overview

Simple dice games are compact, social, and perfect for low-tech downtime. They also help practice mental math and probability intuition.

## Game 1: High or Low (2d6)

**Goal:** Guess whether the total will be 7 or higher.

### Rules
- Roll 2 dice.
- Call **HIGH** (7-12) or **LOW** (2-6).
- Keep score over 10 rounds.

### Decision Flowchart

```diagram flowchart
START: Choose HIGH or LOW
STEP: Roll 2d6
STEP: Total is 7-12? → HIGH wins
STEP: Total is 2-6? → LOW wins
END: Score 1 point, next round
```

---

## Game 2: Target 9 (2d6)

**Goal:** Hit exactly 9 to score.

### Rules
- Each player rolls 2d6 once per round.
- A 9 scores 2 points.
- A 7 or 8 scores 1 point.
- Anything else = 0.

### Scoring Flowchart

```diagram flowchart
START: Roll 2d6
STEP: Total = 9? → Score 2 points
STEP: Total = 7 or 8? → Score 1 point
END: Score 0 points
```

---

## Game 3: Five-in-a-Row (1d6)

**Goal:** Be the first to roll five 5s.

### Rules
- Roll one die repeatedly.
- Each 5 earns a tally.
- First to 5 tallies wins.

## Skill Builder: Mental Math Drill

Roll 2d6 and multiply the total by:
- 2
- 3
- 5

Example:
```
Roll: 4 + 6 = 10
10 × 3 = 30
```

---

## Notes

- Use dice games to keep morale high.
- Small, repeatable rules make them easy to teach.
- Add a timer for faster play.
