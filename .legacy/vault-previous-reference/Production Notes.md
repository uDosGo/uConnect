## Core Intent

*Aussie Rock Rhapsody* is part concert, part drama, part community ritual.

The show moves from cheeky, truthful banter to genuine emotional weight without tipping into melodrama. Humour comes from character — never sketches, never caricature.

Songs are not “numbers.” They are story beats.

---

## Opening Sequence

- Band enters first. No announcement. No overture.
- They plug in, tune up, argue levels.
- Characters drift in during this.
- Hierarchy is intentionally unclear at first.
- First song begins when ready — not when announced.
- BELLA speaks first. One sentence. Then music.
- DAD appears fleetingly (ideally silhouette). Audience may not consciously register it.

The opening is the whole show in miniature.

---

## Music Rules

- All songs performed live.
- Songs do not illustrate story — they are the story.
- No character announces a song.
- Dialogue leads naturally into music.
- Lyrics remain original with minor contextual bracketed adjustments if required.

---

## Tone & Performance Style

- Naturalistic performances.
- Humour emerges from truth.
- Emotional range: laugh-out-loud → gut punch.
- Avoid sentimentality.
- Practical, funny-because-it’s-true warmth.
- Scenes flow seamlessly into songs.

## Character Function Notes

### Jimmy
Earnest. Not bright. Leaves, loses, returns, grows.

### Bella
Emotional centre. Precise, warm, grounded. Chooses herself.

### Sister Girl
Observer. Sharp, cheeky. Cultural wisdom with comic timing.

### Davo
Heart and humour. Physical comedy. Fully self-realised.

### Dad
Anchor. Taciturn. Presence in absence.

### Evie
Catalyst. Direct. No redemption arc required.

### Niko (Island Boy)
Not comic relief.
Audience surrogate and gentle mirror.
Outsider who loves Australia and sees it clearly.

Appears:
- Dam
- Road before finale
- Wings during finale

He resolves nothing. He names what others can’t.
His exit is right, not tragic.

---

## Dialogue & Humour Rules

### Puns

- Never explained.
- Never flagged.
- Land or don’t. Move on.

Dad: deliberate (defence mechanism).
Jimmy & Davo: accidental / misused.

One beat. Next line.

### Malapropisms

Rare.
Character-specific.
Never corrected on stage.

### Famous Australian Lines

Used only when earned.
Never cheap reference gags.

Example:
“A dingo’s got my baby” — played as lonely absurdity, not a punchline.

### Crocodile vs Shark (Milkbar)

Brief. Private exchange.
Unresolved.
Nobody else listens.
Disappears after landing.

## Themes

- Legacy vs identity
- Love vs silence
- Country, culture, community
- Gender, visibility, chosen family
- Homecoming without regression
- Land as healer
- Self-acceptance
- “Nothing really matters” as existential joy

Women are the architecture of the story.
Men are warm and loveable.
This is structural, not a joke.

---

## Ending Philosophy

- No neat resolutions.
- Nobody “gets” anyone.
- Jimmy & Bella share possibility, not promise.
- Finale is about presence, not fixing everything.
- Everyone in the same place, knowing who they are.

That is enough.

## Staging & Transitions

- Skeletal, transformable set.
- Minimal scene changes.
- Scrolling digital backdrop carries geography.
- Ensemble functions as:
  - Community chorus
  - Ancestors
  - Memory figures
  - Elsewhere presences

Ghosts may linger post-scene.

---

## Movement & Choreography

Not traditional musical theatre.

Movement is character-rooted.
Stylised but organic.

Incorporate where meaningful:
- Drag performance language
- Haka energy
- Country line dance
- 80s Australian dance language

---

## Lighting & Sound Design

### Lighting

- Warm bush palette.
- Spotlight for intimacy.
- Drag / dreamworld = hyper-real neon.
- Flame bursts during major rock peaks.

### Sound

Ambient Australian textures:
crickets, wind, windmill clinks, distant thunder, curlew, dingo.

Underscore monologues with instrumental Aussie rock motifs.

---

## Costume, Props & Visual World

### Costume

Authentic 80s rural + bold 80s pop.
Drag transformation is complete and glamorous.

Cultural identity handled respectfully with appropriate advisors.

### Props

Milkbar crates, straw bales, wire fence + physical gate, disco ball, festoon lighting, Hills Hoist silhouette.

Physical props may be simplified with LED support.

## Digital Backdrop Bible

Single continuous painted landscape.
Scrolls RIGHT → LEFT as story progresses.

Style:
Ettamogah Pub meets Ginger Meggs.
Affectionate realism.
Not photorealistic.

Joins between locations matter.

Major pause points:
- Farm
- Dam
- Main Street / Milkbar
- Highway
- City
- Country
- Pub / Rec Hall
- Return to Farm (rain transformation)
- Full Panorama Finale

Rain is the only major weather event.
It is transformational.
