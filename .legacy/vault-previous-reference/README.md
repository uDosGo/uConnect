# Survival Knowledge

**Category Focus**: Essential skills and information for meeting basic needs and handling emergencies.

## What Belongs Here

Practical, actionable knowledge for:
- **Emergency preparedness** - Disaster readiness, survival kits
- **First aid** - Medical emergencies, basic treatment
- **Food and water** - Finding, purifying, storing essentials
- **Shelter** - Emergency housing, weatherproofing, staying safe
- **Basic tools** - Multi-purpose tools, improvisation, repairs

## Subcategories

### first-aid/
Medical emergencies and basic healthcare:
- CPR and basic life support
- Wound care and bleeding control
- Fractures, sprains, and injuries
- Common illnesses and treatments
- Emergency childbirth
- Poison and overdose response
- Medical supply lists

### food-water/
Essential nutrition and hydration:
- Water purification methods
- Finding water sources
- Food preservation techniques
- Foraging for edible plants
- Emergency food supplies
- Calorie requirements
- Long-term food storage

### shelter/
Protection from elements:
- Emergency shelter construction
- Weatherproofing techniques
- Fire building and safety
- Temperature regulation
- Site selection
- Tool-free shelter options

### tools/
Essential survival tools:
- Multi-tool selection and use
- Knife skills and maintenance
- Fire-starting methods
- Rope and knot techniques
- Basic repair skills
- Tool improvisation

## Content Guidelines

### ✅ Include
- **Practical**, step-by-step instructions
- **Proven** techniques with safety warnings
- **No-equipment** or minimal-equipment solutions
- **Regional** variations when relevant
- **Safety** precautions and risks

### ❌ Exclude
- Extreme survival scenarios (rare edge cases)
- Military/tactical content
- Weapons or combat techniques
- Overly complex techniques requiring specialized equipment
- Unverified or dangerous advice

## Contributing

When adding survival knowledge:
1. **Verify** information from reliable sources (Red Cross, CDC, wilderness experts)
2. **Include** safety warnings and when to seek professional help
3. **Test** instructions for clarity (can someone follow without prior knowledge?)
4. **Cite** sources when possible
5. **Update** regularly as best practices evolve
