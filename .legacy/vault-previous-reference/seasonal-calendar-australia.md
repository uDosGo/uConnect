# Australian Seasonal Calendar & Gardening Guide

**Category:** Reference - Planning & Timing
**Priority:** HIGH - Essential for food production
**Region:** General Australian (adjust for local climate)
**Difficulty:** Beginner

---

## Overview

Australia's seasons are opposite to the Northern Hemisphere, and climates vary dramatically from tropical north to temperate south. This guide provides month-by-month planning for gardening, foraging, and preparation.

**Climate Zones (simplified):**
- **Tropical** (Darwin, Cairns) - Wet/Dry seasons
- **Subtropical** (Brisbane, northern NSW) - Mild winters
- **Temperate** (Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide, Perth) - Four distinct seasons
- **Cool** (Hobart, alpine areas) - Cold winters

---

## Month-by-Month Guide

### JANUARY (Summer Peak - Hot & Dry)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 25-33°C, wet season peak      ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 21-29°C, humid, storms        ║
║ Temperate         ║ 18-28°C, dry, fire risk HIGH  ║
║ Cool              ║ 12-22°C, pleasant, can be dry ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Water deeply, early morning (reduce evaporation)
- Mulch heavily (retain moisture, cool soil)
- Harvest: Tomatoes, capsicum, eggplant, zucchini, cucumber, beans, sweetcorn
- Plant (Tropical/Subtropical): Pumpkin, melon, sweet potato
- Plant (Temperate/Cool): Last chance for quick crops (radish, lettuce, Asian greens)
- Shade cloth over delicate plants

**Foraging (Temperate):**
- Blackberries (weed but edible)
- Elderberries (make syrup, cordial)
- Beach plants (samphire, pigface fruit)

**Preparation:**
- Check fire plan (bushfire season PEAK)
- Water storage topped up
- Emergency kit reviewed
- Heat wave preparation (cool room, fluids, check on elderly)

**Wildlife:**
- Snakes active (warm weather)
- Bees swarming (call beekeeper if unwanted swarm)
- Birds nesting (avoid trimming trees)

---

### FEBRUARY (Late Summer - Still Hot)

**Weather:**
```
Similar to January, slightly cooling toward end
Fire risk remains HIGH
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Continue watering (drought stress peak)
- Harvest: Last tomatoes, capsicum, continue beans/zucchini
- Plant (All zones): Start autumn crops (broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage seedlings)
- Plant (Temperate/Cool): Carrot, beetroot, silverbeet, spinach
- Collect seeds from best plants (tomatoes, capsicum, beans)
- Prune: Nothing (too hot, stresses plants)

**Foraging:**
- Figs (trees around old homesteads)
- Late blackberries
- Rosehips (for tea, vitamin C)

**Preparation:**
- Preserve summer harvest (tomato sauce, pickles, jam)
- Dry herbs (basil, oregano, thyme)
- Check water tanks (may need top-up if dry)

**Events:**
- Harvest festivals (local regions)

---

### MARCH (Autumn Begins - Cooling)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 24-31°C, wet season ending    ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 19-27°C, pleasant             ║
║ Temperate         ║ 15-24°C, mild, some rain      ║
║ Cool              ║ 10-18°C, crisp mornings       ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant (All zones): Broad beans, peas, onion, garlic, leafy greens
- Plant (Temperate/Cool): Last brassicas (broccoli, cabbage, kale)
- Harvest: Pumpkins (before frost), last capsicum
- Prepare beds for winter crops (compost, mulch)
- Reduce watering (cooler, less evaporation)
- Divide perennials (rhubarb, chives, etc.)

**Foraging:**
- Mushrooms starting (after rain - ONLY if expert!)
- Olives (around Mediterranean-climate areas)
- Late figs

**Preparation:**
- Firewood collection (if heating with wood)
- Check roof/gutters (rain coming)
- Plant winter vegetables for continuous harvest
- Preserve pumpkins (cool, dry place - lasts months)

**Wildlife:**
- Echidnas active (preparing for winter)
- Migrating birds arriving (honeyeaters, etc.)

---

### APRIL (Autumn Deepens - Mild)

**Weather:**
```
Cooling, increased rainfall (southern states)
Tropical: Dry season established
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant: Garlic cloves (must be in by ANZAC Day - April 25)
- Plant: Snow peas, sugar snap peas, broad beans
- Plant: Asian greens (bok choy, pak choy, mizuna)
- Harvest: Pumpkins cured, tomatoes finished
- Mulch: Heavy layer (winter weed suppression)
- Prune: Deciduous fruit trees starting to drop leaves (wait until bare)
- Citrus: Fruit ripening (lemons, mandarins)

**Foraging:**
- Mushrooms (pine, field - EXPERT ONLY)
- Warrigal greens (native spinach, coastal)
- Native cherries (some species)

**Preparation:**
- ANZAC Day traditions (biscuits, dawn service)
- Autumn clean-up (gutters, yard)
- Check heating systems (test before needed)
- Plant cover crops (green manure for winter)

**Events:**
- Easter (variable date - March/April)
- School holidays (mid-month)

---

### MAY (Late Autumn - First Frosts)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 22-30°C, dry season, pleasant ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 15-23°C, mild, some rain      ║
║ Temperate         ║ 10-18°C, first frosts possible║
║ Cool              ║ 6-14°C, frosts common         ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant: Last chance for garlic, onions, broad beans
- Protect tender plants from frost (fleece, cloches)
- Prune: Deciduous fruit trees once fully dormant
- Harvest: Citrus continues, last capsicums before frost
- Rake leaves (compost or mulch)
- Plant: Winter-flowering natives for bees

**Foraging:**
- Mushrooms continue (wet areas)
- Watercress (clean streams only)
- Native limes (some species)

**Preparation:**
- Firewood stacked, dried, covered
- Winter clothing out (check for moth damage)
- Hot water system check (before heavy use)
- Draught-proof windows/doors

**Wildlife:**
- Wombats preparing for winter
- Some birds migrating north
- Possums seeking warm roof spaces (check exclusions)

---

### JUNE (Winter Begins - Cold & Wet)

**Weather:**
```
Shortest day: June 21 (Winter Solstice)
Southern states: Cold, wet
Northern states: Pleasant, dry
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant (All zones): Not much! Winter rest period
- Plant (Frost-free areas): Lettuce, Asian greens, coriander
- Harvest: Brassicas (broccoli, cabbage, cauliflower), leafy greens
- Prune: Finish deciduous fruit tree pruning
- Mulch: Top up (weed suppression, soil protection)
- Plan: Next season's garden (seed catalogs!)
- Compost: Turn piles, add autumn leaves

**Foraging:**
- Very limited (winter dormancy)
- Watercress (streams)
- Some mushrooms (wet forests)
- Seaweed (after storms, for garden)

**Preparation:**
- Longest nights (enjoy cozy evenings)
- Midwinter celebrations (solstice June 21)
- Check on elderly neighbors (cold weather)
- Heating costs peak (manage energy use)

**Events:**
- Queen's Birthday (second Monday, long weekend)
- School holidays (end of month)

---

### JULY (Mid-Winter - Coldest)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 19-28°C, dry, perfect weather ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 12-21°C, cool nights          ║
║ Temperate         ║ 7-15°C, cold, wet, frosts     ║
║ Cool              ║ 3-11°C, snow in mountains     ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant: Very little (except frost-free zones)
- Harvest: Winter greens (kale, silverbeet, cabbage)
- Prune: Roses (cut back hard in coldest areas)
- Prepare: Beds for spring (add compost, plan layout)
- Protect: Tender perennials from frost
- Indoors: Start seeds (tomatoes, capsicum for early spring)

**Foraging:**
- Winter greens (chickweed, dandelion leaves - pesticide-free areas)
- Seaweed (storms wash up nutrient-rich)

**Preparation:**
- Flu season peak (check health)
- Stay warm (layer clothing, heat one room)
- Check pipes (burst risk if freezing)
- Order seeds for spring (don't wait until sold out!)

**Wildlife:**
- Many animals less active (conserving energy)
- Whales migrating north (east coast)
- Snow in alps (ski season)

---

### AUGUST (Late Winter - Warming Begins)

**Weather:**
```
Days lengthening noticeably
Wattles start flowering (sign of spring)
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant (Temperate/Cool): Potato tubers, onion seedlings
- Plant (All zones): Snow peas (last chance)
- Start seeds indoors: Tomatoes, capsicum, eggplant (8 weeks before last frost)
- Prune: Summer-flowering shrubs
- Fertilize: Spring bulbs emerging
- Harvest: Last of winter brassicas
- Prepare: Spring beds (dig in compost)

**Foraging:**
- Early wattle flowers (not edible, but sign of spring)
- Watercress continues
- Some early mushrooms

**Preparation:**
- Spring cleaning (traditional time)
- Air out house (winter stuffiness)
- Check garden tools (sharpen, repair before rush)
- Plan spring projects

**Events:**
- End of ski season
- Spring approaching (anticipation building)

### SEPTEMBER (Early Spring - Variable)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 23-32°C, building humidity    ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 16-24°C, warming, spring rain ║
║ Temperate         ║ 10-18°C, variable, frosts end ║
║ Cool              ║ 6-14°C, slow warming          ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant (After last frost): Tomato seedlings, beans, zucchini
- Plant (All zones): Carrot, beetroot, lettuce, Asian greens
- Harvest: Last broad beans, start peas
- Fertilize: Fruit trees (before blossoms set)
- Weed: Spring growth explosion (get on top early!)
- Mulch: Before weeds seed
- Divide: Perennials (iris, agapanthus)

**Foraging:**
- Asparagus (wild, roadsides - know your plants!)
- Spring greens (young nettles, chickweed)
- Mushrooms (after spring rain)

**Preparation:**
- Daylight saving starts (first Sunday October, but prepare)
- Allergies (pollen season begins)
- Garden burst (busy time!)
- Bee activity increasing (check hives)

**Events:**
- Father's Day (first Sunday)
- School holidays (end of term 3)
- Football finals (AFL)

---

### OCTOBER (Mid-Spring - Growing Season)

**Weather:**
```
Warming, variable rainfall
Windy (spring winds)
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant (All zones): Everything! Tomatoes, capsicum, eggplant, beans, zucchini, cucumber, pumpkin, melon
- Harvest: Last peas, start lettuce, radish
- Stake: Tomatoes early (easier before large)
- Fertilize: Heavy feeders (tomatoes, pumpkin)
- Water: Establishment of seedlings
- Pest watch: Aphids, caterpillars emerging
- Mow: Lawn growing rapidly

**Foraging:**
- Spring asparagus
- Warrigal greens (new growth)
- Lemon myrtle leaves (native herb)

**Preparation:**
- Daylight saving begins (first Sunday)
- Longer evenings (enjoy outdoors)
- Snake activity increasing (warm days)
- UV increasing (sun protection)

**Events:**
- Melbourne Cup (first Tuesday, public holiday VIC)
- School holidays (brief break)

---

### NOVEMBER (Late Spring - Warming Fast)

**Weather:**
```
╔════════════════════╦═══════════════════════════════╗
║ REGION            ║ CONDITIONS                    ║
╠════════════════════╬═══════════════════════════════╣
║ Tropical          ║ 25-33°C, storm build-up       ║
║ Subtropical       ║ 19-27°C, humid, storms start  ║
║ Temperate         ║ 13-22°C, pleasant, warming    ║
║ Cool              ║ 9-18°C, spring in full        ║
╚════════════════════╩═══════════════════════════════╝
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant: Last chance for tomatoes (8+ weeks to harvest)
- Plant: Successive sowings (beans, lettuce every 2 weeks)
- Harvest: First zucchini, lettuce, radish
- Water: Increase frequency (warming, evaporation)
- Mulch: Essential (heat coming)
- Pest control: Caterpillars, aphids, snails
- Thin seedlings: Crowded plants compete

**Foraging:**
- Early mulberries (start ripening)
- Native fruits (lilly pilly, some species)
- Purslane (weed, but edible and nutritious)

**Preparation:**
- Summer prep (cooling systems, shade cloth)
- Water storage check (summer demand coming)
- Fire season approaching (clean up yard)
- Christmas planning begins

**Wildlife:**
- Baby birds fledging (watch for fallen)
- Bees very active (pollination peak)
- Snakes regularly sighted (caution)

---

### DECEMBER (Summer Begins - Hot)

**Weather:**
```
Longest day: December 21-22 (Summer Solstice)
Heat building, storm season (northern)
```

**Garden Tasks:**
- Plant: Heat-lovers (basil, eggplant - if not done)
- Plant: Successive crops (beans, lettuce, coriander)
- Harvest: Zucchini, beans, first tomatoes, lettuce
- Water: Daily for pots, deeply for beds (early morning)
- Shade cloth: Over tender plants
- Pest watch: Fruit fly (trap, exclude, remove fallen fruit)
- Mow: Regular (but higher in heat - 5cm+)

**Foraging:**
- Mulberries (peak season)
- Early blackberries (warm areas)
- Beach succulents (pigface, samphire)

**Preparation:**
- Christmas (planning, shopping, cooking)
- School holidays (long summer break starts)
- Bushfire season official start (Dec 1 many areas)
- Heat waves (prepare cooling, hydration)

**Events:**
- Summer solstice (longest day Dec 21-22)
- Christmas Day (Dec 25, public holiday)
- Boxing Day (Dec 26, public holiday)
- New Year's Eve (Dec 31)

## Regional Variations

### Tropical (Darwin, Cairns)

**Wet Season** (November - April):
- High humidity, afternoon storms
- Cyclone risk (especially Jan-March)
- Mango season (Sept-Jan)
- Plant wet-season crops (sweet potato, taro)

**Dry Season** (May - October):
- Perfect weather (20-30°C, low humidity)
- Best planting time
- Bushfire risk increases (late dry)
- Tourism peak

---

### Subtropical (Brisbane, Northern NSW)

**Summer** (Dec-Feb):
- Hot, humid, afternoon storms
- Great growing season
- Watch for fruit fly

**Winter** (Jun-Aug):
- Mild, can grow year-round (frost-free)
- Slow growth (cooler temps)
- Perfect weather for humans!

---

### Temperate (Sydney, Melbourne, Adelaide, Perth)

**Four distinct seasons:**
- Spring: Variable, rapid growth
- Summer: Hot, dry (Perth driest)
- Autumn: Mild, harvest time
- Winter: Cold, wet (Melbourne coldest)

**Frost dates** (approximate):
- Last spring frost: Sept-Oct
- First autumn frost: April-May

---

### Cool (Hobart, Mountains)

**Short growing season:**
- Last frost: October-November
- First frost: March-April
- Focus on cool-season crops
- Quick-maturing varieties essential
- Greenhouse extends season

---

## Traditional Seasonal Markers

**Aboriginal Seasons** (vary by region):
- Not just 4 seasons
- 6+ seasons in many areas
- Based on plant flowering, animal behavior, weather patterns
- Example (Noongar, Perth area):
  - Birak (Dec-Jan): Hot, dry
  - Bunuru (Feb-Mar): Hottest
  - Djeran (Apr-May): Cooling
  - Makuru (Jun-Jul): Coldest, wet
  - Djilba (Aug-Sep): Warming, wet
  - Kambarang (Oct-Nov): Wildflowers
