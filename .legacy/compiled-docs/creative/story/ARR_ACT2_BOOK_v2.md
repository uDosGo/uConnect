## Production Note — Act Two
> The interval is not a break. It's a breath.  
> Act Two begins with JIMMY already home. Already failing to close the gate.  
> The women carry Act Two. The men catch up.  
> The gate stays open until the last possible moment.

# Act Two

---

## Scene 1 — Who Can It Be Now?

*BACKDROP: BELLA's house. A screen door. Net curtains. Morning light.*

*JIMMY stands at the door. He's been here a while. He knocked once. He knows she's in there.*

*Inside — BELLA is very still. She heard the knock. She is not moving.*

*JIMMY knocks again. Lightly.*

**JIMMY:** *(to the door, quietly)* Bella. I know you're home.

*Silence from inside.*

**JIMMY:** I can see the kettle's on. The steam is—

*Inside, BELLA silently removes the kettle from the heat.*

**JIMMY:** I just want to talk.

*Silence.*

**JIMMY:** I'm not going anywhere.

*Inside:*

**BELLA:** *(very quietly, to herself)* You went somewhere before just fine.

---

### Song 2-01: WHO CAN IT BE NOW? (Men at Work)

*Full song*

*JIMMY sings the verses from outside — pleading, slightly pathetic, entirely genuine.*  
*"Let me inside... I need to talk to you..."*  
*BELLA inside — "Go 'way, don't come round here no more..."*  
*They sing at the same door from opposite sides.*  
*It's funny. It's also genuinely sad.*  
*JIMMY eventually sits down on the step. He's not leaving.*  
*BELLA eventually sits down on the other side of the door. She's not opening it.*  
*They're two feet apart.*

---

*After the song. JIMMY still on the step. BELLA still inside.*

*A long beat.*

**JIMMY:** *(to the door)* He left me the place.

*Silence.*

**JIMMY:** The farm. He left it to me. I didn't... I didn't think he would.

*A very long silence from inside.*

**BELLA:** *(through the door, quiet)* Of course he did.

**JIMMY:** Yeah.

*Another beat.*

**BELLA:** *(through the door)* There's not happy Jan. The gate's still open.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** I know.

**BELLA:** Go close it.

*He stands up. He goes.*

*Inside, BELLA sits quietly for a moment.*

*Then she makes another cup of tea.*

## Scene 2 — Do You See What I See / We Can't Be Beaten

*BACKDROP: The pub. Afternoon. One other bloke at the bar who isn't listening.*

*JIMMY and DAVO. Day drinking, but not dangerously — just the kind that happens when you need to sit somewhere that isn't home.*

*DAVO has a beer. JIMMY has a beer. There's a third beer that appeared as if by magic.*

**DAVO:** You look terrible.

**JIMMY:** Thanks.

**DAVO:** Like, genuinely. You look like the farm ate you.

**JIMMY:** The farm might be eating me.

**DAVO:** You sleeping?

**JIMMY:** Enough.

**DAVO:** Eating?

**JIMMY:** There's a pie in the fridge from last Tuesday.

**DAVO:** That's not— mate. That's not eating.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** When did you know? About yourself. In the city.

*DAVO goes quiet for a moment. This is territory they haven't walked into before.*

**DAVO:** I think I always knew. I just needed somewhere nobody was watching.

**JIMMY:** And now?

**DAVO:** Now everyone's watching and I don't mind anymore. Mostly.

**JIMMY:** That's... that's good, mate. That's really good.

**DAVO:** *(looks at him)* Sometimes you just have to be different. Like actually different. Not just... pretending you're not what you are.

*A long beat. JIMMY looks at his beer.*

**JIMMY:** I think I'm in love with Bella.

**DAVO:** *(flat)* Mate. Everyone knows that. The sheep know that. George at the milkbar knows that. There's a woman in Tamworth who's never met you who probably knows that.

**JIMMY:** Have I always been that obvious?

**DAVO:** You stared at her across a paddock for three years without saying a word. "Obvious" is doing a lot of work there.

**JIMMY:** Right.

*Beat.*

**DAVO:** What are you gonna do about it?

**JIMMY:** I'm working on it.

**DAVO:** Work faster. She's not gonna wait forever.

**JIMMY:** I know.

**DAVO:** She waited a pretty long time already.

**JIMMY:** I know.

**DAVO:** *(drinks)* Lock it in, Eddie.

**JIMMY:** *(looks at him)* Did you just—

**DAVO:** Felt right.

---

### Song 2-02a: DO YOU SEE WHAT I SEE? (Hunters & Collectors)

*Part song*

*JIMMY and DAVO seeing the same things differently — the town, the past, the future.*  
*DAVO sees possibility. JIMMY sees guilt. The song holds both.*  
*"Do you see what I see?"*  
*They're looking at the same paddock. They're seeing different things.*

---

### Song 2-02b: WE CAN'T BE BEATEN (Rose Tattoo)

*Part song*

*The pivot. From introspection to action.*  
*Two blokes, shoulder to shoulder, deciding to be okay.*  
*It's funny and it's real and the band lifts.*  
*"Shoulder to shoulder we're gonna stand."*  
*The pub erupts briefly. The one other bloke at the bar raises his glass.*

---

*After the songs. They're still at the bar. Something lighter between them now.*

**DAVO:** You're gonna be alright, you know.

**JIMMY:** You sure?

**DAVO:** No. But I'm saying it anyway.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** *(looks at his beer)* How much can a koala bear?

**DAVO:** *(confused)* What?

**JIMMY:** Nothing. Just. A lot's happened.

**DAVO:** Yeah. *(pause)* Yeah it has.

*They clink beers.*

## Scene 3 — Solid Rock

*BACKDROP: Country. Not a location — a feeling. Red earth. Ancient. Infinite. The sky bigger than everything.*

*BELLA and SISTER GIRL out bush. Not hiking. Not sightseeing. Just here, because this is where they're from.*

*SISTER GIRL sits on a rock. BELLA stands, looking at something far away.*

**SISTER GIRL:** You know what I like about out here?

**BELLA:** What.

**SISTER GIRL:** Nothing needs explaining.

*BELLA almost smiles.*

*A long, easy silence. The kind you can only have in certain places.*

**BELLA:** My Nan said the land remembers everything. Every step, every lie, every song.

**SISTER GIRL:** She wasn't wrong.

**BELLA:** She said the men wrote down stories. But the women... the women remembered them.

*SISTER GIRL nods. She picks up a small stone. Turns it over.*

**SISTER GIRL:** You know they were gonna sell this block? Council. Last year. Before the community pushed back.

**BELLA:** I know.

**SISTER GIRL:** They never stop.

**BELLA:** No. But neither do we.

*She places her hand flat on the dirt.*

**BELLA:** I'm not his echo. I'm the sound.

---

### Song 2-03: SOLID ROCK (Goanna)

*Full song — including didgeridoo intro*

*BELLA's moment. Slow. Spacious. Anchored.*  
*The backdrop shows Country — not picturesque, not tourist-brochure. Real. Ancient. Alive.*  
*SISTER GIRL behind her. The ensemble — ancestors, community — visible in the wings.*  
*"You're standin' on solid rock / You're standin' on sacred ground / You're livin' on borrowed time."*  
*This is not played for drama. It is the drama.*  
*She belongs here in a way that has nothing to do with any man at all.*

---

*After the song. The two women sit together in the quiet.*

**SISTER GIRL:** Not happy Jan.

**BELLA:** *(pause)* About what?

**SISTER GIRL:** Any of it. Everything they took. Everything they still try to take.

*Beat.*

**BELLA:** Yeah. *(quietly)* But we're still here.

**SISTER GIRL:** Sixty thousand years.

**BELLA:** And counting.

*They sit. The land holds them.*

---

## Scene 4 — My Girl / Take Me Back

*BACKDROP: The farm. Working now — slowly coming back to life under JIMMY's hands. The fence mended. The bore pump running.*

*JIMMY is at work but not present. His mind is elsewhere. The dam. The ridge. BELLA laughing at something he can't remember anymore.*

*He stops. Sits on a fence post.*

*From the wings — faintly — DAD's bass line. The memory of him.*

*JIMMY sits with it.*

---

*The B&S Ball — night. String lights. Dust and music and everyone they grew up with.*

*JIMMY didn't think BELLA would be here.*

*BELLA didn't think he'd come back.*

*They see each other across the room.*

*Neither moves yet.*

**JIMMY:** *(to himself)* Didn't think you'd be here.

*He walks over. She watches him come.*

**BELLA:** Didn't think you'd come back.

*Pause.*

**JIMMY:** Couldn't stay away. Not from... this.

**BELLA:** This place? Or your guilt?

**JIMMY:** Both, maybe.

*BELLA sighs.*

**BELLA:** You ever realise someone was holdin' space for you — and you just never stepped into it?

**JIMMY:** Yeah. I realise it every time I see you.

**BELLA:** And still you wait for an invite?

*Long silence.*

**JIMMY:** When my dad died, I thought I'd feel free. But all I felt was small. Like I didn't know who the hell I was without him to push against.

**BELLA:** You're not small. You just stood in his shadow too long.

**JIMMY:** I wanted to be better. For you.

**BELLA:** Be better for you. Then maybe I'll give ya a crack.

**JIMMY:** *(quietly)* Can I still sing to you?

**BELLA:** Sing if you mean it. Not 'cause it's easy.

*JIMMY sits. Pulls out his guitar. A soft strum — the opening to "My Girl."*

*Lights shift.*

---

### Song 2-04a: MY GIRL (Hoodoo Gurus)

*Full song*

*JIMMY — dramatic, over-the-top, genuinely feeling every word.*  
*"My girl don't love me at all / anymore."*  
*The song is about what he imagines. What's in his head. The love that existed mostly in that enormous space between them.*  
*Memory drifts through the dam, the ridge, BELLA laughing.*

---

### Song 2-04b: TAKE ME BACK (Noiseworks)

*Part song — overlapping / intercut with My Girl*

*Regret and longing interweave.*  
*"Take me back to you, it's been a long time."*  
*They sing toward each other — not at each other — separate on stage.*  
*It's beautiful. It aches. It doesn't resolve.*

---

*After the songs. They're still there. Neither has moved.*

**BELLA:** *(quietly)* I heard you.

**JIMMY:** Yeah?

**BELLA:** I always hear you, Jimmy. That's the problem.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** Is it still a problem?

*She looks at him for a long moment.*

**BELLA:** Ask me again when you've closed the gate.

*She walks away. He watches her go.*

---

## Scene 5 — Throw Your Arms Around Me / Burn For You

*BACKDROP: Split stage. Two locations at once.*

*JIMMY — alone on the farm, sitting near where his dad used to work.*  
*BELLA — alone on Country, in the same golden light.*

*They don't know they're singing at the same moment.*

---

### Song 2-05a: THROW YOUR ARMS AROUND ME (Hunters & Collectors)

*Partial duet — split stage*

*BELLA's half. Tender. The heart still open, boundaries still intact.*  
*"I will come for you at night time / I will raise you from your sleep."*  
*She sings it like a possibility, not a promise.*

---

### Song 2-05b: BURN FOR YOU (INXS)

*Part song — 0:42 to 2:45*

*JIMMY's half. Desperate but honest.*  
*"Tilt my hat at the sun / and the shadows they burn dark / Light me and I'll burn for you."*  
*The unified moment: both sing "tilt my hat at the sun..." — love is possible, if you really want it.*  
*They don't hear each other. But the audience does.*  
*It's beautiful. It doesn't resolve.*

---

*After the songs. The split stage holds a moment.*

*Then — from one side of the stage to the other — a single light bridge.*

*Neither of them walks across it.*

*Not yet.*

## Scene 6 — Dreamworld

*BACKDROP: The B&S Ball, later in the evening. Festoon lights. The cheap disco ball. Purple haze.*

*EVIE returns. She arrives the way she always does — like a weather event. No apologies.*

*BELLA sees her first.*

**BELLA:** You came back.

**EVIE:** *(checks reflection in a compact)* Did I leave?

**BELLA:** You left everyone's life pretty thoroughly.

**EVIE:** *(snaps compact shut)* I didn't leave yours.

*Beat.*

**BELLA:** No. I suppose you didn't.

*EVIE looks around the hall.*

**EVIE:** He's here.

**BELLA:** I know.

**EVIE:** He looks like a man who's been working very hard and sleeping very badly.

**BELLA:** Yeah.

**EVIE:** *(beat)* How long are you going to make him wait?

**BELLA:** Until he deserves it.

**EVIE:** *(a beat of genuine respect)* Fair enough.

*They stand together for a moment, watching the room.*

**EVIE:** I spent so long being the loudest person in every room. I forgot how to hear myself.

**BELLA:** *(quiet)* You're allowed to change. You don't have to be the punchline if it stops being funny.

**EVIE:** *(looks at her)* That's surprisingly decent of you.

**BELLA:** Don't push it.

*SISTER GIRL appears with two cans of Solo.*

**SISTER GIRL:** You gonna pout into that compact 'til sunrise or actually dance?

**EVIE:** It's called stage presence. You wouldn't understand — you've got natural charm.

**SISTER GIRL:** Flattery? Are you sick?

**EVIE:** Maybe. *(beat)* Save me a spot on stage. I'm bringing glitter and grief.

*SISTER GIRL cracks her Solo. They clink cans.*

**SISTER GIRL:** They do "chicken star-tay, just like bar-lay" at that restaurant in the city. Very rustic, apparently.

**EVIE:** *(laughing)* That's just chicken in sauce.

**BELLA:** Everything's rustic when white people make it.

---

### Song 2-06: DREAMWORLD (Midnight Oil)

*Full song*

*EVIE and BELLA together — or rather, each in her own power, but side by side.*  
*EVIE: the dreamworld of escape, performance, running away — "your dreamworld is just about to end."*  
*BELLA: the dreamworld of waiting for someone to save you — also ending.*  
*Together: something new. Neither needs rescuing. Neither is leaving on someone else's terms.*  
*SISTER GIRL at keys — she's decided to play tonight. That's a big moment.*  
*No boys required.*  
*"End — your dreamworld is just about to end / Fall — your dreamworld is just about to fall."*

---

*After the song. The three women. Something settled between them.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to EVIE)* You're alright, you know.

**EVIE:** Don't tell anyone. I have a reputation.

**SISTER GIRL:** "You're terrible, Muriel." *(beat)* But you're alright.

*EVIE laughs — a real one.*
