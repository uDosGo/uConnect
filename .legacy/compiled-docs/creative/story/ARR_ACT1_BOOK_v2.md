## PRODUCTION NOTE
> The band walks on first. No announcement. No overture. They plug in, tune up, argue about levels.  
> SISTER GIRL adjusts the keys twice. DAD finds his bass lead on the third try.  
> Characters drift in. The first song begins when it's ready — not when it's announced.  
> The music starts first.

## SCENE 1 — THIS IS AUSTRALIA

*BACKDROP: Red dirt. A wide flat horizon. Heat haze. A farmhouse in the distance. A rusted water tank. A ute. A Hills Hoist with one good arm.*

*The stage is dark. The band walks on one by one. DAD settles on bass. JIMMY picks up guitar. DAVO takes the drums with the focus of a man who has sat behind a kit since he was twelve. SISTER GIRL slides onto keys, checks the settings, adjusts one thing, leaves the rest.*

*They tune up. Mess around. Check levels. It feels unrehearsed — because it is, a little.*

*BELLA walks to the front of the stage. She doesn't pick up an instrument. She stands and looks out at the audience for a long moment.*

*Then EVIE appears at the edge of the light — she's not in the band, she's moving through the space like she owns it.*

**BELLA:** *(quietly, to no one in particular)* This is Australia.

*The band starts. Gentle at first.*

---

### SONG 1-01: SOUNDS OF THEN — *This Is Australia* (Gangajang)
*Full song — 3:56*

*As the song plays, EVIE drifts through the space, watching everyone. JIMMY clocks her from behind his guitar. DAVO misses a cue watching her. DAD doesn't look up from the bass line — he never does.*

*NIKO appears briefly at the edge of the stage. Watching. He's been here before, sort of. He moves on.*

*By the final chorus, all characters are visible. The farm is alive. This is who we are.*

---

*The song ends. The band lets it breathe. JIMMY sets his guitar down and wipes his face with his shirt. BELLA and SISTER GIRL settle near the fence line. DAD is already doing something to a piece of machinery that will never be fixed.*

**JIMMY:** Bloody hot.

**DAVO:** *(still behind the drums)* Yeah.

**JIMMY:** You gonna sit there all day?

**DAVO:** Maybe.

*DAD looks up from the machinery.*

**DAD:** Jimmy. That bore's not gonna fix itself.

**JIMMY:** Yeah righto.

**DAD:** *(back to the machinery, almost to himself)* Bore me to tears, this bloody thing.

*JIMMY heads over. DAVO follows, reluctantly.*

*Over at the fence line:*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to BELLA, watching the boys)* Those two are planning something stupid.

**BELLA:** Always are.

**SISTER GIRL:** Jimmy keeps looking over here.

**BELLA:** I know.

**SISTER GIRL:** You could put him out of his misery.

**BELLA:** Why would I do that?

**SISTER GIRL:** *(laughs)* Cold.

**BELLA:** Not cold. Just... I know how this goes. Farm boy gets restless, chases something shiny, comes back eventually. Or doesn't.

**SISTER GIRL:** You reckon he's leaving?

**BELLA:** Everyone leaves.

*SISTER GIRL studies her sister.*

**SISTER GIRL:** Not everyone.

*BELLA doesn't respond. The weight of staying when everyone else goes.*

## SCENE 2 — WORKING CLASS MAN / FLAME TREES

*BACKDROP: The farm. Close on the fence line. Red dust. A windmill turning slowly.*

*DAD and JIMMY work the fence. Physical, repetitive, familiar. There's a whole conversation in the silence.*

**DAD:** You're doing it wrong.

**JIMMY:** It's holding, isn't it?

**DAD:** Till the next roo goes through it.

*JIMMY doesn't respond. Works in silence. DAD checks the wire.*

**DAD:** This wire's under a lot of strain. *(beat)* Like me.

*JIMMY glances at him. DAD is already looking elsewhere.*

**DAD:** Your mother used to say you got my stubbornness. *(pause)* Reckon she was right about that one.

**JIMMY:** Dad—

**DAD:** Just saying.

*More silence. The discomfort sits between them.*

### SONG 1-02a: WORKING CLASS MAN (Jimmy Barnes)
*Part song — 0:00 to 1:30*

*DAD's story. Vietnam. The land. The life he chose — or the life that chose him.*  
*JIMMY works alongside him. He knows every word. He's heard it his whole life.*  
*"He did his time in Vietnam / Still mad at Uncle Sam / He's a simple man / With a heart of gold."*

---

*The song fades. They keep working.*

**JIMMY:** You ever think about going somewhere else?

**DAD:** *(doesn't stop working)* Like where.

**JIMMY:** I dunno. Just. Somewhere else.

**DAD:** This land doesn't go somewhere else.

**JIMMY:** I know that.

**DAD:** Then you've answered your own question, haven't ya.

*JIMMY looks at the horizon. The flame trees along the property line are going off — orange and red in the heat.*

**JIMMY:** Davo reckons there's work in the city.

**DAD:** Davo reckons a lot of things.

**JIMMY:** What's wrong with wanting something different?

**DAD:** *(beat)* Nothing. Long as you know what you're walking away from.

*He wipes his hands on his shorts.*

**DAD:** Your grandfather planted those trees.

**JIMMY:** I know.

**DAD:** His grandfather before him.

**JIMMY:** *(quietly)* I know, Dad.

*A long beat. DAD looks at the trees — the most we'll see of what's inside him.*

**DAD:** You wanna go, go. Just know what you're leaving.

*He goes back to the fence. JIMMY stands looking at the trees.*

*(Under his breath, looking at the gate — still open)*

**DAD:** *(to himself)* Tell him he's dreaming.

## SCENE 3 — ORIGINAL SIN / ARE YOU OLD ENOUGH

*BACKDROP: The dam. Late afternoon gold. Eucalypts. Water that doesn't move.*

*BELLA and SISTER GIRL have been here all afternoon. SISTER GIRL is on her phone. BELLA is watching the water.*

**SISTER GIRL:** He was looking at you again.

**BELLA:** Was he.

**SISTER GIRL:** From the fence line. Trying to be subtle.

**BELLA:** He's not great at subtle.

**SISTER GIRL:** He's not great at a lot of things.

*Beat.*

**BELLA:** He's alright.

**SISTER GIRL:** *(looks up from phone)* "He's alright." She says. About the man who looks like that.

**BELLA:** *(still watching the water)* What do you want me to say.

**SISTER GIRL:** I want you to say something that isn't "he's alright."

*BELLA almost smiles.*

**BELLA:** He's very alright.

**SISTER GIRL:** *(goes back to phone)* Progress.

*Beat.*

**SISTER GIRL:** His mob's been here three generations. Thinks that makes them local.

**BELLA:** *(dry)* We've been here sixty thousand years.

**SISTER GIRL:** *(without looking up)* Yep.

*Beat.*

**SISTER GIRL:** They put cheese on everything, you know. White people.

**BELLA:** I know.

**SISTER GIRL:** Everything. I went to that girl Kylie's for dinner. She put cheese on the pasta AND THEN put more cheese on top of that cheese.

**BELLA:** That's just lasagne.

**SISTER GIRL:** It wasn't lasagne. It was pasta with cheese with more cheese on top. She called it "rustic."

*BELLA throws a pebble in the water.*

**BELLA:** *(quietly)* But he does look like that.

*SISTER GIRL doesn't look up. But she smiles.*

*NIKO passes by — guitar on his back, no particular destination. He notices them.*

**NIKO:** G'day.

**BELLA:** G'day.

*He keeps walking. SISTER GIRL watches him go.*

**SISTER GIRL:** New Zealander.

**BELLA:** Yep.

**SISTER GIRL:** They always look a bit lost.

**BELLA:** They always are a bit lost.

*Part song — 0:00 to 1:29*

*NIKO's song. He sings it like a question he already knows the answer to.*  
*BELLA watches him pass. SISTER GIRL pretends not to.*

**BELLA:** *(evenly)* Hey.

**JIMMY:** Hot day.

**BELLA:** Yeah.

*SISTER GIRL looks up from her phone. Looks at JIMMY. Looks at BELLA. Looks back at JIMMY. The look on her face is the face of someone watching a dog try to open a door.*

**JIMMY:** *(walks over)* You been here long?

**BELLA:** We live here.

**JIMMY:** Yeah. Right. I meant — today. At the dam.

**BELLA:** A while.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** I'm Jimmy.

**BELLA:** I know who you are, Jimmy.

*Another beat. JIMMY smiles. It's a good smile. Even SISTER GIRL has to acknowledge this privately.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(under her breath)* Sixty thousand years.

*BELLA shoots her a look.*

### SONG 1-03b: ORIGINAL SIN (INXS)
*Full song — 3:47*

*"Dream on white boy. Dream on black girl. Then wake up to a brand new day."*  
*JIMMY and BELLA. The tension is enormous. They don't touch. They don't need to.*  
*SISTER GIRL watches the whole thing like she's seen this movie before. She has.*

---

*After the song. JIMMY is gone. BELLA is still at the dam.*

**SISTER GIRL:** So.

**BELLA:** Don't.

**SISTER GIRL:** I'm not saying anything.

**BELLA:** Good.

*Beat.*

**SISTER GIRL:** I'm just saying. Original sin and all that.

**BELLA:** *(standing up)* Let's go home.

*They go. SISTER GIRL glances back once where JIMMY was standing.*

## SCENE 4 — THE MILKBAR

*BACKDROP: Main street. A ceiling fan that does nothing. A fridge full of Fanta. Formica tables. Dagwood dogs in a bain-marie. A cigarette ad on the wall: "You've come a long way, baby."*

*JIMMY and DAVO are in a booth. Two meat pies. DAVO has already eaten his. He's eyeing JIMMY's.*

**DAVO:** You gonna eat that?

**JIMMY:** Yes.

**DAVO:** You've been looking at it for ten minutes.

**JIMMY:** I'm thinking.

**DAVO:** About the pie?

**JIMMY:** About stuff.

*DAVO steals a corner. JIMMY doesn't stop him.*

**DAVO:** Your dad again?

**JIMMY:** He wants me to stay.

**DAVO:** He didn't say that.

**JIMMY:** He didn't have to.

*Beat.*

**DAVO:** I've been thinking the same thing actually.

**JIMMY:** Staying?

**DAVO:** *(looks at him)* No mate. The other thing. Going.

**JIMMY:** Yeah?

**DAVO:** There's stuff I want to... figure out. That I can't figure out here.

**JIMMY:** What stuff?

**DAVO:** *(beat)* Just. Stuff.

*JIMMY nods. Doesn't push it. That's what mates do.*

*Meanwhile — across the milkbar, BELLA and SISTER GIRL are at the counter.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to the owner, deep in negotiation)* It has vegetables in it.

**GEORGE:** *(Greek-Australian, warm)* A Chiko Roll is not a meal, sweetheart.

**SISTER GIRL:** It has cabbage.

**GEORGE:** Cabbage is not vegetables when it's inside a Chiko Roll.

**BELLA:** *(to SISTER GIRL)* Just get two.

**SISTER GIRL:** I want it to be a meal though.

**BELLA:** It can be a meal and also be two.

*SISTER GIRL considers this philosophy.*

**SISTER GIRL:** ...fair enough.

*BELLA glances over at JIMMY's booth. Doesn't linger.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(without turning)* He's looking at you again.

**BELLA:** I know.

**SISTER GIRL:** You're not looking back.

**BELLA:** I know.

**SISTER GIRL:** Crocodile or shark?

**BELLA:** What?

**SISTER GIRL:** If you had to choose. Crocodile or shark.

**BELLA:** That's not a real choice. You're in the water or you're not.

**SISTER GIRL:** You're always in one or the other. That's the point.

**BELLA:** ...crocodile.

**SISTER GIRL:** Wrong.

**BELLA:** How is that wrong—

**SISTER GIRL:** Shark doesn't follow you onto land.

*(Beat.)*

**BELLA:** *(considers)* ...crocodile.

**SISTER GIRL:** You just said crocodile.

**BELLA:** I'm sticking with it.

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to George)* Two Chiko Rolls thanks, George.

*The door opens. EVIE walks in.*

*Everything adjusts slightly — not theatrically, just... she's the kind of person a room notices. She goes to the counter. Doesn't look at anyone.*

*JIMMY and DAVO both look. Both look at each other. Both look back.*

**DAVO:** *(very quietly)* Strewth.

**JIMMY:** *(very quietly)* Yeah.

**DAVO:** She's not from here.

**JIMMY:** No.

**DAVO:** You can tell.

**JIMMY:** *(still looking)* Yep.

*SISTER GIRL looks at EVIE. Looks at BELLA. Raises her eyebrows exactly one millimetre.*

**BELLA:** *(very quietly)* Don't.

**SISTER GIRL:** *(very quietly)* Buckle up, sis.

*EVIE orders something. Leans on the counter. Still hasn't looked at JIMMY.*

**DAVO:** You should say something.

**JIMMY:** Like what.

**DAVO:** I dunno. Something.

**JIMMY:** You say something.

**DAVO:** It's not me she's gonna want to talk to.

*JIMMY looks at DAVO. DAVO shrugs — an honest shrug.*

*JIMMY stands up. Smooths his shirt. Walks to the counter.*

*Beat. DAVO watches. EVIE still hasn't looked up.*

**JIMMY:** G'day.

**EVIE:** *(without looking up)* Hi.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** You, uh. Passing through?

**EVIE:** *(looks up. Takes him in.)* Maybe.

*Back at the booth, DAVO nods to himself like a coach whose player just executed a play correctly.*

*At the counter, BELLA watches EVIE. EVIE hasn't noticed BELLA.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to BELLA, eating Chiko Roll)* Told ya.

### SONG 1-04: THE BOYS LIGHT UP (Australian Crawl)
*Full song — 4:30*

*JIMMY and DAVO at their most alive — young, loud, ridiculous. The song is them exactly.*  
*EVIE watches from the counter with the expression of someone watching a nature documentary.*  
*She's amused. She's interested. She knows exactly where this is going.*  
*BELLA and SISTER GIRL finish their Chiko Rolls. SISTER GIRL mouths along to the song without realising.*  
*BELLA sees this and says nothing.*

---

*After the song.*

**EVIE:** *(to JIMMY)* You live here?

**JIMMY:** Farm. Just out of town.

**EVIE:** What's in the city?

**JIMMY:** *(caught off guard)* What?

**EVIE:** If you could go. What would you do there?

*JIMMY has never been asked this directly. He thinks about it genuinely.*

**JIMMY:** I dunno. Everything?

*EVIE smiles. It's a dangerous smile.*

**EVIE:** I'm going back on Thursday.

*She picks up her order and walks out.*

*DAVO appears at JIMMY's shoulder.*

**DAVO:** What'd she say?

**JIMMY:** She's going back to the city Thursday.

**DAVO:** And?

*JIMMY looks at the door.*

**JIMMY:** And nothing. And — I dunno. She asked me what I'd do there.

**DAVO:** What'd you say?

**JIMMY:** I said "everything."

**DAVO:** *(nodding slowly)* That's actually not bad, mate.

*Beat. JIMMY looks over his shoulder. BELLA is gone. Her stool at the counter is empty.*

## SCENE 5 — EVIE

*BACKDROP: The highway. Red dirt giving way to bitumen. Then the first signs of a city — water towers, a servo, a McDonald's that seems impossible this far out.*

*JIMMY is on the highway with a bag that is too small for what he's doing. EVIE pulls up in a car. He gets in.*

*They drive. We see JIMMY's face as the city gets closer. Wonder. Fear. Excitement. He looks like a kid at Christmas who's not sure he deserves the presents.*

*EVIE drives like she's done this a thousand times. Because she has.*

*Back on the side of the road — DAVO watches the car go. He wasn't invited. He wasn't not invited either. He stands there until the car is a dot.*

*Then he picks up his own bag.*

*Then he starts walking.*

*Back at the farm: DAD looks up from the machinery. The ute is gone. He looks at the gate — still open. Then goes back to what he was doing.*

## SCENE 6 — YOU SHOOK ME ALL NIGHT LONG

*BACKDROP: The city. All of it. Bright, loud, relentless. A sharehouse. A bar. A street at 2am that doesn't know it's 2am.*

*Weeks have passed. JIMMY has adapted faster than he expected — or thinks he has.*

*He's at a bar. Animated. Talking to people. He's found his feet socially, which surprises him most of all.*

*EVIE watches him with genuine affection. She's not performing anything here.*

*Across the bar — DAVO has also arrived. He's at a different table, talking to a man named ALEX. Really talking. The kind of talking he doesn't do at home. Nobody notices DAVO's conversation. That's the point.*

**EVIE:** You're not what I expected.

**JIMMY:** What'd you expect?

**EVIE:** Less real, I think.

**JIMMY:** I'm pretty real.

**EVIE:** *(smiles)* Yeah. You are.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** This place is unbelievable. I didn't know it was like this.

**EVIE:** You can stay, you know. No law says you have to go back.

*JIMMY looks at her.*

**JIMMY:** My dad—

**EVIE:** Your dad's a grown man.

*A long beat. JIMMY looks at his drink.*

**JIMMY:** *(quietly)* He did his time in Vietnam, you know. Still mad at Uncle Sam. He never talks about it but you can — you can see it sometimes. When he doesn't know you're watching.

*EVIE looks at him differently. Just for a moment.*

**EVIE:** *(gently)* He'll be alright.

**JIMMY:** Yeah.

*He almost believes it.*

**JIMMY:** *(looks down)* ...I dunno.

**JIMMY:** I had it.

**EVIE:** You definitely had it.

**JIMMY:** *(beat)* The city's expensive.

## SCENE 7 — RUN TO PARADISE

*BACKDROP: The city spiralling. Faster. Louder. The farm in the scrolling background getting smaller, then gone entirely.*

*Meanwhile — BELLA is on a bus. She has her bag. She has SISTER GIRL on the phone.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(on phone)* Why are you doing this.

**BELLA:** Because I want to.

**SISTER GIRL:** You want to chase a man who doesn't know you're coming.

**BELLA:** I'm not chasing anyone. I want to see the city.

**SISTER GIRL:** You've been to the city.

**BELLA:** *(pause)* I want to see if he's okay.

*A long pause from SISTER GIRL's end.*

**SISTER GIRL:** He's fine. They're always fine.

**BELLA:** I'll be back in a few days.

**SISTER GIRL:** Bring me back a fridge magnet. One of those Opera House ones.

**BELLA:** You've got three of those already.

**SISTER GIRL:** Four is better.

*BELLA hangs up. Looks out the bus window at the city approaching.*

*Back home, SISTER GIRL puts the phone down and looks at the empty house.*

**SISTER GIRL:** *(to herself, very quietly)* A dingo's got my baby.

*She picks up the remote. Turns on the TV.*

*Back in the city — JIMMY and EVIE. The flat. Takeaway containers. Music on. The world has shrunk to this room.*

*DAD on the farm — a letter on the kitchen table he hasn't opened. Bills.*

*DAVO somewhere in the city, finding himself quietly, piece by piece.*

## SCENE 8 — HOWZAT

*BACKDROP: City street. A window. The inside of a flat.*

*BELLA got the address from DAVO — she called him, not JIMMY. DAVO gave it with zero explanation and hung up. He knew.*

*She knocks. No answer. The door's not latched.*

*She pushes it open.*

*JIMMY and EVIE inside. Not in a compromising position — just entirely, completely in their own world. Takeaway containers. Music on. Two people who have forgotten there's anything outside this flat.*

*JIMMY looks up and sees BELLA.*

*The world stops.*

**JIMMY:** Bella.

*EVIE reads the room instantly. She is very good at reading rooms.*

**BELLA:** *(quietly)* Sorry. Door was open.

**JIMMY:** No — yeah — come in—

*BELLA doesn't come in.*

*She looks at EVIE. EVIE looks back. No hostility. Just honesty.*

**BELLA:** *(to JIMMY)* You look good.

**JIMMY:** I... yeah. You came to the city.

**BELLA:** Just passing through.

*She's not just passing through. He knows it. She knows he knows it.*

*A long beat.*

**BELLA:** Your dad's got the bore pump going. Fence on the south side needs work.

**JIMMY:** Right.

**BELLA:** Thought you should know.

*She goes.*

*EVIE says nothing. She watches JIMMY watch BELLA leave.*

## SCENE 9 — DUMB THINGS

*BACKDROP: The farm. Still. The windmill not turning. A phone on a kitchen table.*

*JIMMY gets the call at the flat. We don't hear what's said. We see his face.*

*He sits down slowly on the edge of the bed.*

*EVIE comes to the doorway.*

**EVIE:** *(quietly)* Who was it?

**JIMMY:** Mick from next door.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** Dad had a stroke. Last night. They found him this morning.

*EVIE crosses to him. Sits beside him.*

**EVIE:** Oh, Jimmy.

**JIMMY:** He was alone.

*A long silence.*

**JIMMY:** She told me about the bore pump. Bella told me about the bore pump and I stayed here anyway.

**EVIE:** Jimmy—

**JIMMY:** I knew. And I stayed.

*He stands up. He needs to move.*

**JIMMY:** I do dumb things. I always — I lost my shirt in this city. Literally. And I stayed for what? I've done all the dumb things.

*He's not performing grief. It's too real for that. It's the quiet kind.*

*From somewhere in the room — from the walls, maybe — a line from something his dad once said:*

**DAD:** *(recorded bass line, faint)* "Don't bore yourself trying to be someone you're not."

*JIMMY stands very still.*

*Then he goes to get his bag.*

---

### SONG 1-09: DUMB THINGS (Paul Kelly)
*Full song — 2:40*

*Paul Kelly knows. The song knows.*  
*JIMMY in the flat as the city carries on outside — sirens, music, someone laughing in the street.*  
*The farm backdrop slowly reappears in the scroll. The land is pulling him back.*  
*EVIE watches from the doorway. She loves him, in her way. She also knows what comes next.*

**EVIE:** You don't have to decide tonight.

**JIMMY:** Yeah I do.

**EVIE:** I'm not going to tell you to stay.

**JIMMY:** I know.

**EVIE:** And I'm not going to say I'll come with you.

**JIMMY:** *(looks at her)* I know that too.

*A beat. Something honest between them.*

**EVIE:** He was proud of you. Whatever it looked like.

**JIMMY:** You didn't know him.

**EVIE:** No. But I know you.

## SCENE 10 — ONE WORD

*BACKDROP: The city at night. A bus stop. The highway. Then stars.*

*DAVO is already at the bus stop when JIMMY gets there. Bag packed. Ready.*

**JIMMY:** How'd you know?

**DAVO:** Mick called me too.

*Beat.*

**JIMMY:** You don't have to come.
